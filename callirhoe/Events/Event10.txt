Erie Canal opens after over 1000 workers die building it.

26/10/1825

<p>On October 26, 1825, the Erie Canal opened, eight years after construction commenced. This engineering marvel would have enormous impacts on the future of American work, including spurring ever-greater industrialization, helping cement the Great Lakes states as a center of American industrialization, and ensuring New York would be the long-term center of American commerce. It also came at a cost of over 1000 dead workers. </p><p><a href="http://www.lawyersgunsmoneyblog.com/2013/10/this-day-in-labor-history-october-26-1825">Read whole article</a></p>
