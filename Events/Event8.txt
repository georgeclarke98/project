Frank Little lynched in Butte.

01/08/1917

<p>On August 1, 1917, Frank Little, a veteran IWW organizer, was dragged out of his hotel in Butte, Montana by company thugs and lynched. His murder, one of the most famous killings of a labor organizer in American history, demonstrated the lengths to which mining companies were willing to go to keep their company towns under control. </p><p><a href="http://www.lawyersgunsmoneyblog.com/2013/08/this-day-in-labor-history-august-1-1917">Read whole article</a></p>
